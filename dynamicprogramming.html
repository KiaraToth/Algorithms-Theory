The Dungeon of
Dynamic Programming

An oral-exam trial in five chambers

The Examiner's Summons

You are an apprentice algorist, and the time has come to descend. Five sealed chambers lie below, each holding the mathematical soul of one dynamic-programming algorithm. To pass each gate you must do what the real examiner demands at the blackboard:

  • Write the problem in mathematical form and say what you are searching a solution for.
  • Explain what the algorithm is for and where it's used.
  • State the optimal substructure, the recurrence, the pseudocode, and the running time — in order, the way you must recite it.

Every rune sealed in gold you must learn word for word. Tap Why? anywhere for the reasoning, and Say it in plain words to turn any formula into spoken English. Reach the bottom, and the Examiner will pick one algorithm at random and test you — exactly like the real thing.

Built entirely from your Dynamic Programming notes. Examples are kept small only so the tables stay readable.

The Descent

Each door opens onto one algorithm. Master a chamber to claim its rune and unlock the way down. You may revisit any opened chamber to revise.

Collect all 5 runes to unlock the final trial.
